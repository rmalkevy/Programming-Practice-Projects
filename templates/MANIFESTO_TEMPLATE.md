# MANIFESTO

> **Lab 42 — Life, The Universe, And Everything.**
> Copy this template into your project as `MANIFESTO.md` and fill it out *before* you write code.
> Delete the helper text in italics. Keep the headings exactly as they are.

---

## The Question

*A single sentence, framed simply enough that a non-engineer understands it. The question must be specific, defensible, and yours. If you can't fit it on one line, you haven't found it yet — keep going.*

> Example questions:
> *Can a Ukrainian high-school student plan and watch a real LEO satellite pass without ever leaving their browser?*
> *What does an honest dating app look like?*
> *Can my grandmother manage her medication with one app, no fuss?*
> *What does a 1970s-style flight computer feel like in 2026?*
> *Could I make a free CS textbook genuinely better than the paid ones?*

---

## The Premise

*Two to three paragraphs. Why this question? Why now? What already exists in this space? Where does it fall short? What is missing — that you specifically can build?*

*Be honest about prior art. Be honest about your motivation. Be honest about why you, specifically, are the right person to ship this answer.*

---

## The Stack

*What you're building this with, and why.*

*Include: language(s), framework(s), hosting / deployment target, key libraries. One paragraph is fine.*

*Pick boring tech for the spine, exciting tech for the soul. Don't pick three new frameworks; the project will outlast its tooling only if the tooling is one you can hold.*

> Example stack note:
> *TypeScript + Next.js (App Router) for the web frontend; Python + FastAPI for the inference backend; Hugging Face Spaces for the model demo; Cloudflare Pages for the marketing page; Plausible for analytics.*

---

## The Constraints

*This is the hardest section to write — and the most important.*

*List 4–6 things you will explicitly **not** do in this project. Examples: scope cuts, features deferred, audiences not served, platforms not supported, problems not solved.*

*A project without explicit constraints is a project that will not ship.*

> Example constraints:
> 1. *No native mobile app — web only, mobile-friendly.*
> 2. *No multi-tenant accounts — single-user-per-deployment.*
> 3. *No multi-language UI — Ukrainian and English only.*
> 4. *No real-time collaboration — async only.*
> 5. *No AI features beyond the embedded model — no fine-tuning, no agents.*
> 6. *No payment integration — free for MVP.*

## The Plan

*A short, dated plan — at the granularity that's useful to you. Recommended: 3 weeks (Week 1: question + spike; Week 2: vertical slice; Week 3: polish, demo, defense). If you're doing the longer version, scale accordingly.*

*Include at least one mid-checkpoint milestone where the project is **shipped, even if rough**. The vertical slice is non-negotiable.*

---

## The Definition of Shipped

*One paragraph. What does "shipped" mean for this specific project?*

*Be concrete:*
- *URL someone can visit?*
- *APK someone can install?*
- *Package someone can `pip install`?*
- *Open-source repo with a GitHub Release?*
- *Tagged binary in a Docker image?*

*A real human who is not you must be able to use the result. That is the floor.*

---

## The User

*Who is the person you are building this for? One sentence.*

*Resist the urge to write "developers" or "everyone" or "people who care about X." Write a real person, ideally one you know. Someone you can show the working version to during the showcase. Someone whose feedback you'll take seriously.*

*If your user is yourself, that's allowed — say so explicitly. Tools you build for yourself are valid.*

> Example users:
> *My grandmother (in Lviv, age 73, smartphone-comfortable, takes 4 medications daily, sometimes forgets the morning ones).*
> *Me, on a long train ride, when I want to study but can't connect to wifi.*
> *Volunteer ambulance medics in eastern Ukraine, working in vehicles with intermittent connectivity.*

---

## The Risks

*List 2–3 things that could cause this project to fail. For each, name the spike experiment that will discover whether the risk is real, and a date by which you'll have run that spike.*

*Risks usually live in: external APIs that may not exist as advertised, performance budgets that may not be met, ambiguous requirements that may not converge, hardware that may not arrive on time.*

---

## The Showcase Pitch

*A 60-second elevator pitch. Write it now (before you've built anything). Re-read it weekly. Update it when reality changes the project.*

> Recommended structure:
> 1. *Sentence 1: who you built it for.*
> 2. *Sentence 2: what it does.*
> 3. *Sentence 3: what makes it different.*
> 4. *Sentence 4: where they can use it right now.*
> 5. *Sentence 5: the next thing you'll add.*
